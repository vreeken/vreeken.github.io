About Me

I enjoy the logical and scientific side of life. Math and science have rules and definitive answers to their questions, as opposed to interpretive answers found in art and literature. I find structure, reason, and organization necessary for my sanity, and have found solace in programming.

I've been programming as a hobby and career for over 20 years. I got hooked on BASIC back in ~2000 and things spiraled out of control from there. I experimented with many languages in college, but the immediate satisfaction that came from javascript UI control, and ease of publishing content to the world led me more toward web development. Flash/Actionscript was a wild ride (while it lasted) that taught me a lot of fundamentals through game development, but the logic and architecture of back end programming is what calls to me the most.

While PHP has plenty of inconsistencies and other issues, I love it (so long as I get to use v7+); and the Laravel Framework has only increased my love of the language. The same can be said for Javascript and the Vue.js Framework (v2). I find I achieve the most productivity when I couple a Laravel back end with a Vue.js front end. I've tested a few different database solutions, but the availability of MySQL keeps me coming back to it.

My current position heavily utilizes AWS services, so I'm certified with AWS Solutions Architect; and I've become very familiar with RDS, ECR, ECS, EC2, CloudWatch, Docker, and CI/CD via Github Actions.

While I'm not tinkering with code you can usually find me playing video games or saving the world in my latest Dungeons and Dragons game.
